ATSlay

An ATS score you can actually argue with — because it shows its work.

RoleSolo Developer
StackPython · NLP
StatusLive, Dockerized
The Problem

Most ATS resume checkers hand you a percentage and nothing else — a black-box score with no explanation of what to fix. That's frustrating for candidates and, frankly, not very honest engineering: if you can't show your work, the number isn't trustworthy.

ATSlay exists to fix that: same core idea as a resume-matching tool, but with the reasoning made visible.

The Approach

Instead of calling an external LLM API to "judge" a resume, I built the scoring engine from real NLP fundamentals — so the output is deterministic, explainable, and free to run at scale.

  • TF-IDF vectorization of both the resume and job description
  • Cosine similarity to compute a match score between the two vectors
  • Porter stemming to normalize word forms before comparison (so "managing" and "managed" count as a match)
  • The output highlights exactly which keywords are missing — not just a score
  • Full auth system with scan history, so users can track improvement over multiple resume versions

Outcome & Honest Limits

The scoring pipeline works end to end and is genuinely explainable — you can trace exactly why a score came out the way it did. What it doesn't yet handle well: resumes with heavy graphic/table-based formatting (parsing accuracy drops), and it doesn't account for years-of-experience weighting the way a human recruiter would. Both are on my list, not hidden from the pitch.
